The Crisis We Face

Water shortage and deforestation represent two of the most critical environmental emergencies of our time

Water Crisis

Water scarcity affects over 40% of the global population, with projections suggesting that by 2025, two-thirds of the world could face water-stressed conditions.

  • 2.2 billion people lack access to safely managed drinking water
  • By 2030, global water demand will exceed supply by 40%
  • Agriculture uses 70% of global freshwater

Forest Destruction

Deforestation continues at a devastating pace, with an area equivalent to 27 soccer fields lost every minute worldwide.

  • 10 million hectares of forests lost annually
  • Amazon has lost 17% of tree cover in 50 years
  • 80% of terrestrial species live in forests

The Deadly Connection

Water scarcity and deforestation are interconnected crises that accelerate each other

Forests Regulate Water Cycles

Trees absorb rainfall, recharge groundwater, and prevent floods. Deforestation disrupts this cycle, leading to irregular rainfall and droughts.

Loss of Forests = Loss of Water

The Amazon generates 20% of Earth's oxygen and influences rainfall across South America. Cutting trees reduces evapotranspiration, drying up regional climates.

Water Scarcity Accelerates Forest Loss

Droughts increase wildfires and weaken trees, making them vulnerable to pests and disease, creating a vicious cycle of destruction.

Why Should You Care?

Without forests, rainfall patterns collapse, leading to longer droughts

Without water, agriculture fails, causing food shortages and economic crises

Biodiversity loss accelerates, pushing species toward extinction
