Policy & Global Actions

International agreements and NGO initiatives driving change worldwide

International Agreements

Paris Climate Agreement

195 countries committed to limiting global warming to 1.5°C, requiring massive reforestation efforts.

Status: Active - 189 countries ratified

UN Sustainable Development Goals

Goal 6 (Clean Water) and Goal 15 (Life on Land) directly address water scarcity and deforestation.

Deadline: 2030 - 6 years remaining

CBD Global Framework

30x30 commitment to protect 30% of land and oceans by 2030.

Adoption: December 2022 - Implementation phase

Leading NGO Initiatives

World Wildlife Fund (WWF)

Leading forest conservation efforts and fighting illegal wildlife trade.

  • Protected 1.2 billion acres of land
  • Reduced deforestation by 50% in target areas
  • $10 billion mobilized for conservation

Greenpeace

Campaigning against illegal logging and promoting renewable energy.

  • Exposed illegal logging in 50+ countries
  • Secured zero-deforestation commitments
  • Saved 12 million acres of ancient forests

Rainforest Alliance

Certifying sustainable agriculture and forest management practices.

  • 32 million acres under sustainable management
  • 1.2 million farmers trained
  • 85% reduction in deforestation on certified farms

Success Stories

Proof that these solutions work when implemented at scale

Costa Rica's Forest Recovery

Once down to 17% forest cover, Costa Rica restored forest coverage to over 54% through payments for ecosystem services and strict protection laws.

Forest cover: 17% → 54% Biodiversity: Recovered endangered species Economy: $4 billion from eco-tourism

Singapore's Water Independence

Through NEWater recycling, desalination, and water conservation, Singapore achieved water security despite having no natural freshwater sources.

Water recycling: 40% of supply Desalination: 30% of supply Water security: 2060 independence target

Sahel Restoration Success

Niger restored 5 million hectares of degraded land through farmer-managed natural regeneration, improving food security for 2.5 million people.

Land restored: 5 million hectares Food security: 2.5 million people Tree density: 10-fold increase
